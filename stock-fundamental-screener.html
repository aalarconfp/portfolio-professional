Overview

This screener systematically ranks the ~30 most liquid equities on the Bolsa de Valores de Colombia (BVC) by five fundamental ratios, producing a composite score that objectively identifies the top 10 stocks with the best combination of valuation, profitability, yield, and leverage at any given point in time.

It solves a specific challenge in the Colombian market: data accessibility. Unlike US equities, BVC stocks lack a centralized, structured data export. This workflow bridges that gap by combining a custom TradingView screener with Claude AI for structured extraction, feeding a fully automated Power Query / Power Pivot pipeline in Excel.

The dashboard supports period-over-period comparison, tracking which stocks have entered or exited the Top 10 between analysis dates — enabling a systematic, bias-free approach to portfolio rebalancing decisions on the BVC.

Ranking Methodology

Each stock is ranked independently on five fundamental dimensions. The individual ranks are then summed into a composite score — the lower the score, the better the overall fundamental quality. The 10 stocks with the lowest composite scores form the Top 10.

RPG
Price / Earnings
Valuation multiple. Lower = cheaper relative to earnings.
P/VL
Price / Book Value
Asset-based valuation. Lower = trading closer to net assets.
Yield %
Dividend Yield
Income return. Higher = more attractive for income-oriented investors.
ROE %
Return on Equity
Profitability. Higher = management generates more return per peso of equity.
Debt/EBIT
Leverage Ratio
Solvency. Lower = less time needed to repay debt from operating earnings.

Stocks with negative EBIT (ETB, CONCONCRET, ENKA) are excluded from the Debt/EBIT ranking as the ratio is not meaningful. Additional exclusions apply for insufficient trading volume (ELCONDOR, BBVACOL, CNEC, VILLAS, PFVILLAS), ensuring the screener reflects only investable securities.

Data Pipeline

BVC financial data is sourced from a custom saved screener on TradingView, which provides real-time fundamental ratios for Colombian equities across all sectors. Because TradingView does not offer a structured export for this screener, the extraction step uses a screenshot-to-data workflow powered by Claude AI.

Update Workflow

The screener is designed to be refreshed in under five minutes per analysis period, with no structural changes to the workbook. The end-to-end update process:

01
Capture TradingView Screenshot
Open the saved custom screener on TradingView and take two screenshots covering all displayed columns. Save with the analysis date in the filename (e.g., TradingView Screenshoot Data BVC 2026-03-30).
02
Extract Data via Claude AI
Upload the screenshots to Claude. Claude reads the table, normalizes headers, handles edge cases (negative values, missing data, formatting), and outputs a structured Excel-compatible file: best-view-data-[date].xlsx.
03
Update Power Query Source
In the Best Ratio Stock Power Query, update the file path in the M source line to point to the new data file. Validate the query executes without errors.
04
Refresh & Validate Dashboard
Close Power Query and refresh all queries. The PivotTable and ranking dashboard update automatically. Validate that the Top 10 composite scores are correctly sorted and that strategy signals are applied.
05
Archive Period Snapshot via VBA
Run the VBA macro to save the current PivotTable state as a named snapshot in the historical data table. Update the Best Ratio Stock Previous Period query to reference the snapshot, then refresh to display the period-over-period Top 10 comparison.

Dashboard Sections

The workbook is organized into three analytical views, each addressing a distinct phase of the investment selection process — from universe ranking to portfolio decision-making.

View 01
Full Universe Rankings
Complete ranking table of ~30 BVC equities sorted by composite fundamental score. Displays all five ratio ranks, individual ratio values, sector classification, portfolio membership indicators, and analyst strategy signals (Buy / Hold / Sell). Conditionally formatted to highlight Top 10 entries.
View 02
Top 10 Selector
Focused view of the current Top 10 stocks with their composite score breakdown. Includes portfolio-overlap indicators (whether the stock is already held) and dividend event flags — supporting buy/add decisions ahead of ex-dividend dates.
View 03
Period-over-Period Comparison
Side-by-side comparison of the current and previous period's Top 10. Tracks which stocks have entered, remained in, or dropped out of the selection — providing a systematic, evidence-based basis for portfolio rebalancing rather than reactive decisions.
View 04
Historical Snapshots Archive
VBA-generated table accumulating all past analysis periods in a single structured dataset. Enables long-term trend analysis of ranking movements and strategy signal consistency across market cycles.

Business Value

The Colombian equity market presents a structural data challenge: unlike US exchanges, BVC companies lack a single, API-accessible source of clean fundamental data. This screener creates a repeatable, low-cost solution to that problem by combining free-tier TradingView data with AI-assisted extraction — eliminating the need for expensive data subscriptions while maintaining analytical rigor.

For a self-directed investor with BVC exposure, the dashboard replaces subjective stock selection with a disciplined, rules-based process. The composite ranking forces all five fundamental dimensions to be considered simultaneously, preventing over-indexing on any single metric such as yield or P/E alone.

  • Universe coverage — Screens the full investable BVC universe (~30 liquid equities) in a single update cycle
  • Objective ranking — Composite score eliminates selection bias by weighting all five ratios equally
  • AI-powered data bridge — Claude extraction step removes the manual data-entry bottleneck unique to BVC market data
  • Portfolio integration — Cross-references screener output against held positions and upcoming dividend dates
  • Audit trail — VBA snapshot archive preserves every analysis period for back-testing and accountability
  • Rebalancing discipline — Period comparison view surfaces rotation signals based on fundamentals, not price momentum
